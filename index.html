The heart of Central Java, Semarang, with its multicultural history would be the best city for those who love exploring cities with diverse cultural heritage. It’s also one of the largest metropolitan cities in Indonesia, and provides so many compelling itineraries for you to visit.

  • Semarang is traversed by many rivers in the middle of the city just like Venice in Italy.
  • Netherland ever called this city as Venetië van Java or The Venice of Java.
  • This city is also known as Kota Lumpia or Lumpia City. It’s a snack born by acculturation of two cultures, Javanese and Chinese, and a typical food from Semarang.

As one of the huge cities in Indonesia, Semarang is never deserted by tourists all over the world. Let’s learn more about the reasons why.

Why Travel To Semarang?

One of the cities that is quite historic with its legendary culinary. Enjoy the splendor of ancient architecture and learn the richness of cultural acculturation. Even the natural attractions are suitable to be explored without having to spend a lot of money.

This tourist spot has many Instagramable spots, starting from the main building that looks like Borobudur Temple, to the colorful slides that are giant in size. Lawang Sewu

Rp.10.000 - 20.000

Lawang Sewu was formerly the office of the Nederlandsch-Indische Spoorweg Maatschappij (NIS or NISM), the first private railway company in Indonesia. The building is not only magnificent during the day but also charming when night falls. No wonder many make it a place to hang out and cheap night tours.

Saloka Theme Park

Rp.120.000 - 150.000

SALOKA is present as one of the Pesona Indonesia tourist destinations in the form of a family thematic recreation park in Central Java that carries the concept of local wisdom. Located at the intersection between the cities of Semarang, Salatiga, Surakarta and the Special Region of Yogyakarta.

Masjid Jateng

Free

The Great Mosque of Central Java is the largest and grandest and is an icon and pride of the people of the province. This mosque is also one of the largest mosques in Indonesia. One of the hallmarks of this mosque is the six giant hydraulic umbrellas on the terrace of the mosque.

Pantai Marina

Rp.5000 /person

One of the beach attractions that has an exotic view typical of the city of Semarang. In the past, this place was a mangrove forest and pond, but the local government turned it into a recreation area by means of land reclamation. The results of the reclamation of this mangrove forest, now in the form of housing, shops, and offices, on the south coast.

Culture

Semarang Culture Traditions

Dugderan

Tradisi Bulan Ramadan

Dugderan is a tradition where exactly the day before Muslims start fasting, a carnival will be held. The carnival was enlivened by a carnival, a procession of troops in traditional clothes, drum bands, and other traditional arts.

Nyadran

Tradisi Ziarah Kubur

The nyadran tradition is not a strange ritual for the Javanese people, including Semarang. Nyadran is usually done before fasting begins, namely in the month of Ruwah. Muslims will make a pilgrimage to the tomb of their family or ancestors and then clean the grave.

Popokan

Tradisi Syukuran

In Semarang there is a unique tradition that is held on Kliwon Friday in August every year. This tradition is called diaper which is believed to be a repellent against evil and evil. Diapers are also held as a form of gratitude for the blessings of an abundant harvest.
